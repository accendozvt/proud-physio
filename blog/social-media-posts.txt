🦶 Stubborn heel pain that bites with the first steps of the morning? Or a tennis elbow that just will not settle?

Shockwave therapy (ESWT) uses acoustic pressure waves to kick-start healing in tissue that has stopped repairing itself.
For chronic heel pain, it is an evidence-supported option after 3+ months of conservative care.

Article: https://proudphysio.com/blog/blood-flow-restriction-training/
==============================================================================

[Instagram / Facebook post]

💪 Can light weights really build muscle? With blood flow restriction (BFR) training, yes.

A cuff partially restricts blood flow while you train with light loads (around 20 to 40% of your max).
Systematic reviews show muscle growth similar to heavy lifting, a real win when heavy weights are not safe.
It is a valuable tool early after ACL surgery, for painful joints, and for older patients.
BFR must be done under trained supervision with proper screening, never solo from a video.

POST 8 of 20  |  Dizziness and Vertigo: How Physiotherapy Treats BPPV
Article: https://proudphysio.com/blog/vertigo-bppv-physiotherapy/
==============================================================================

[Instagram / Facebook]

😵‍💫 Does the room spin when you roll over in bed or tip your head back? It may be BPPV, one of the most treatable causes of dizziness.

BPPV happens when tiny crystals in the inner ear drift into the wrong canal.
The Epley manoeuvre is a safe, drug-free treatment. A Cochrane review found a single manoeuvre resolves symptoms in just under 80% of people.
No serious side effects, and it can be repeated if vertigo returns.
Important: get assessed first, because not all dizziness is BPPV.

POST 13 of 20  |  Why Your Shoulder Stiffens and Hurts: Frozen Shoulder Explained
Article: https://proudphysio.com/blog/frozen-shoulder-explained/
==============================================================================

Platform: Instagram / Facebook

🧊 Struggling to reach behind your back or lift your arm overhead? Your shoulder may be freezing up.

Frozen shoulder happens when the capsule around the joint thickens and tightens, causing pain and slowly stealing your movement.
It usually passes through three stages, freezing, frozen and thawing, and can last many months.
It is more common between ages 40 and 60, in women, and in people with diabetes or thyroid problems.
The reassuring part: physiotherapy helps ease the pain and coax movement back at the pace your joint allows.

Article: https://proudphysio.com/blog/cervicogenic-headache/
==============================================================================

Platform: Instagram / Facebook

🤕 What if your stubborn one-sided headache is not really coming from your head?

A cervicogenic headache actually starts in the joints, muscles or nerves of your upper neck, then refers pain up into the head.
Clues: it begins at the base of the skull, spreads to one side, and flares after long hours at a desk.
It is easily mistaken for migraine or tension headache, so getting the source right matters.
Physiotherapy, manual therapy, posture correction and neck strengthening can genuinely reduce these headaches.
