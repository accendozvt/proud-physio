Postpartum Care and Physiotherapy: The Missing Piece of Motherhood Recovery

By Proud Physio & Wellness, Kakkanad & Kochi  |  10 June 2026

Bringing a baby into the world changes everything, including your body. Yet while so much attention is given to pregnancy and delivery, the recovery that follows is often overlooked. Many new mothers are told that aches, leaks, and weakness are simply part of motherhood. The truth is that with the right support, your body can recover fully and feel strong again.

This is where postpartum physiotherapy becomes the missing piece. At Proud Physio and Wellness in Kakkanad, we help mothers across Kochi rebuild strength, restore function, and return to the activities they love.

What Happens to the Body After Childbirth

Pregnancy and delivery place enormous demands on the muscles, joints, and connective tissue of the abdomen and pelvis. Common changes include:

  • Pelvic floor weakness, which can lead to leaking when you cough, laugh, or exercise
  • Abdominal separation, known as diastasis recti, where the tummy muscles stay apart
  • Lower back and hip pain from posture changes and feeding positions
  • Reduced core strength that makes lifting and daily tasks harder

None of these are things you simply have to live with. They respond very well to guided, gentle rehabilitation.

How Postpartum Physiotherapy Helps

Pelvic floor rehabilitation

A physiotherapist assesses your pelvic floor and teaches you how to activate and strengthen it correctly. Done right, this resolves most cases of leaking and supports your core from the inside out.

Safe core recovery

We guide you through progressive exercises that close abdominal separation and rebuild deep core strength, without the risk of doing too much too soon.

Pain relief and posture

Hands on therapy and tailored exercises ease back, neck, and hip pain caused by feeding, carrying, and broken sleep.

Every mother recovers at her own pace. A personalised plan, built around your delivery and your goals, is always safer and more effective than generic online routines.

When Should You Start

Gentle recovery can begin within the first few weeks, while more active strengthening usually starts around six weeks after a normal delivery, or later after a caesarean. The best first step is a professional assessment so your plan matches your body.

Home Care for New Mothers

Leaving home with a newborn is not always easy. That is why we offer home care physiotherapy across Kakkanad and Kochi, so you can begin your recovery in comfort while caring for your baby.

Motherhood is demanding enough. With the right physiotherapy support, your recovery does not have to be something you face alone.
