🦶 That sharp jab in your heel with your very first steps of the day? That is the classic sign of plantar fasciitis.

Here is why it happens: the plantar fascia tightens while you rest, so standing suddenly stretches it and the heel protests.
The good news: first-line care is simple and conservative, no injections needed for most people.
Think calf and sole stretching, supportive cushioned footwear, easing high-impact activity, and avoiding barefoot walking on hard floors.
