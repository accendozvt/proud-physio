Tennis Elbow Explained: How Physiotherapy Gets You Back to Your Best

By Proud Physio & Wellness, Kakkanad & Kochi  |  12 June 2026

The name is misleading. Tennis elbow affects far more people who have never picked up a racket than those who have. Painters, cooks, IT professionals, new parents, and gym goers all develop it. If you feel a nagging pain on the outer side of your elbow when you grip, lift, or twist, this article is for you.

What Is Tennis Elbow

Tennis elbow, known medically as lateral epicondylitis, is an overuse injury of the tendons that attach your forearm muscles to the outer elbow. Repeated gripping and wrist movements create tiny strains in these tendons, leading to pain, weakness, and tenderness.

Common Symptoms

  • Pain on the outer elbow that may travel down the forearm
  • A weak or painful grip, for example when holding a cup or shaking hands
  • Discomfort when lifting, twisting, or straightening the wrist
  • Stiffness in the elbow, often worse in the morning

Why Rest Alone Is Not Enough

Many people simply wait for tennis elbow to settle. While rest can ease symptoms for a while, the pain often returns the moment normal activity resumes, because the underlying tendon weakness was never addressed. This is why structured physiotherapy gives far better long term results.

How Physiotherapy Treats Tennis Elbow

Accurate assessment

We first confirm the diagnosis and identify the activities and movement patterns driving your pain.

Pain relief

Hands on therapy, soft tissue work, and activity modification calm the irritated tendon and reduce day to day discomfort.

Progressive loading

The most important part of recovery is carefully strengthening the tendon with graded exercises. This rebuilds capacity so the elbow can handle your work and sport again.

Prevention

We review your technique, ergonomics, and equipment so the problem does not come back.

Most cases of tennis elbow improve significantly with the right loading programme. The key is consistency and expert guidance, not complete rest.
