Paediatric Physiotherapy

Play-based, child-friendly physiotherapy that helps children reach their milestones, build strength and move with confidence, with parents involved at every step.

Playful, milestone-focused therapy for babies and children, at our clinic and in homes across Kochi.

Watching your child grow is one of life's greatest joys, and one of its greatest worries when something seems delayed. Perhaps your baby is not rolling or sitting when other children do, a toddler walks on tiptoes or trips often, or a doctor has mentioned words like developmental delay or cerebral palsy. For parents, knowing where to turn first can feel overwhelming, and the uncertainty is often the hardest part.

Paediatric physiotherapy at Proud Physio & Wellness gives children the specialist support they need, delivered in a way they actually enjoy. Led personally by Dr. Noora Ameen TA (PT), our sessions are built around play, games, toys, songs and movement challenges that feel like fun to a child but are carefully designed therapy underneath. And because parents know their child best, you are involved in every single session, learning exactly how to continue the work at home.

Every Child Deserves a Strong Start

Children are not small adults. Their bones, muscles and nervous systems are still developing, which means both the assessment and the treatment must be different. Our paediatric programmes respect each child's age, temperament and attention span. A frightened or bored child does not learn, so winning trust and keeping sessions joyful is central to how the therapy works.

We also understand that no two families arrive with the same worry. Some come with a clear diagnosis; others simply have a nagging sense that a milestone is late. Both are welcome, and both benefit from an early, careful look by someone who knows exactly what to observe.

Conditions We Help With

Paediatric physiotherapy supports a wide range of movement and development concerns, from the first months of life through the school years. We regularly help children with:

Developmental delay Cerebral palsy Torticollis (tilted neck) Flat feet & gait problems Muscular dystrophy Post-injury recovery in children Coordination difficulties Prematurity-related motor delay Toe walking & posture issues

Because a child's needs change quickly as they grow, we never treat a label in isolation. We look at how your child moves, plays and interacts, alongside their strength, balance and coordination, and shape a programme around the next skill they are ready to reach for.

Why Early Intervention Matters So Much

A young child's brain is at its most adaptable in the first few years of life, forming new connections at an astonishing rate. This is precisely why starting physiotherapy early, sometimes before a formal diagnosis is even confirmed, gives children the very best chance of catching up with their peers. Waiting to see whether a child grows out of a difficulty can mean missing the window when progress comes most easily.

If you are worried about a milestone, do not wait and watch. An early assessment brings either reassurance or a head start, and both are worth having.

What Paediatric Physiotherapy Involves

Every programme begins with understanding your child and your concerns, not with exercise. Here is how a typical journey with us unfolds:

  1. Gentle, thorough assessment

    We observe how your child moves, plays, sits, stands and walks, compare development against expected milestones, and listen carefully to your concerns as a parent.

  2. Play-based treatment plan

    Therapy is woven into games and activities your child enjoys, reaching for toys, obstacle courses, balance games, so every session builds strength, balance and coordination without tears or boredom.

  3. Parent coaching in every session

    We show you exactly what to practise at home and how to build therapy into daily routines like bath time, play time and walking to school, because children improve fastest when practice happens every day.

  4. Milestone tracking and review

    We measure progress against clear developmental goals, celebrate every new skill, and adjust the programme as your child grows and improves.

Support for Parents, Not Just Children

A child's therapy succeeds or struggles at home. That is why we treat parents as partners, explaining what we are doing and why, in plain language, answering every question and providing simple written home programmes. For families whose children find clinic visits difficult, our home care physiotherapy service brings paediatric sessions to homes across Kakkanad, Kochi and Ernakulam, in the environment where your child is most relaxed.

Home sessions can be especially calming for younger children and those who tire quickly. Wherever we treat your child, our aim is the same: to leave you feeling confident, informed and never alone with your worries.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my child needs physiotherapy?

Common signs include not reaching milestones like rolling, sitting, crawling or walking at the expected age, persistent tiptoe walking, frequent falls, a head that tilts to one side, or clumsiness compared to other children. If you are unsure, a single assessment can give you clarity and peace of mind.

Will my child find the sessions frightening or painful?

No. Paediatric sessions are built around play and are never forced. We take time to win each child's trust, work at their pace and keep parents beside them throughout, so most children genuinely enjoy their therapy.

Do you treat children at home in Kochi?

Yes. We offer paediatric physiotherapy home visits across Kakkanad, Kochi and surrounding areas of Ernakulam, which many young children find more comfortable. Call +91 80894 14419 to arrange an assessment.

At what age can physiotherapy begin?

Therapy can begin in the first weeks of life if needed, for example with torticollis or after premature birth. Early intervention is often the most effective, because the young brain adapts so readily, so there is no age too young for an assessment.

How long will my child need to attend?

It depends entirely on the concern and your child's progress. Some children need only a short block of sessions with home practice, while others with ongoing conditions benefit from longer support. After the first assessment we will explain a clear, honest plan and review it regularly as your child grows.
