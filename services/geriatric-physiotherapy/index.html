Geriatric Physiotherapy

Helping older adults stay strong, steady and independent, with compassionate, evidence-based care at our clinic or in the comfort of your home.

Personalised strength and balance rehabilitation for seniors, at our clinic and in your home.

Getting older should not mean giving up the activities you love or the independence you have always valued. Yet for many seniors, everyday tasks like climbing stairs, walking to the shop or simply getting out of a chair slowly become harder. Stiff joints, weaker muscles, poor balance and the quiet fear of falling can gradually shrink a person's world until they stop going out altogether.

Geriatric physiotherapy at Proud Physio & Wellness is designed to reverse that decline. Led personally by Dr. Noora Ameen TA (PT), our programmes focus on restoring strength, sharpening balance and keeping older adults confidently on their feet, whether recovery follows an illness, a fall, surgery, or simply the gradual effects of age itself. The goal is never just to reduce pain. It is to give people back the confidence to live fully.

Conditions We Help With

Ageing rarely brings a single, tidy problem. Most older patients arrive with two or three concerns layered on top of one another, and our job is to untangle them into a plan that makes daily life easier. We regularly treat:

Osteoarthritis & joint pain Balance problems & falls Osteoporosis Post-fracture recovery Parkinson's disease Stroke recovery General weakness & deconditioning Knee & hip replacement rehab Chronic back & neck pain

Because these conditions interact, we never treat a diagnosis in isolation. We assess the whole person, including strength, balance, walking pattern, medications, home environment and daily routine, and then build a programme that fits real life rather than a textbook.

Why Strength and Balance Matter More With Age

From around the age of fifty, adults lose muscle mass steadily each decade unless they actively train to keep it. This gradual loss, known as sarcopenia, is the hidden reason so many seniors feel weaker, tire faster and become unsteady on their feet. The encouraging news is that muscle remains remarkably trainable throughout life. With the right graded programme, even patients in their eighties and nineties build measurable strength within a few weeks, and that strength translates directly into steadier walking, easier stairs and fewer falls.

Falls are not an inevitable part of ageing. They are a treatable, preventable risk that responds strongly to the right physiotherapy.

What Geriatric Physiotherapy Involves

Every programme begins with understanding, not exercise. Here is how a typical journey with us unfolds:

  1. Comprehensive assessment

    We evaluate strength, joint mobility, balance, walking speed and fall risk, review your medical history and medications, and listen carefully to your goals, whether that is walking to the temple, playing with grandchildren or living safely alone.

  2. Personalised treatment plan

    Your programme may combine gentle strengthening, balance retraining, joint mobilisation, pain-relieving manual therapy and safe, graded walking practice, all paced to your comfort and progress.

  3. Fall prevention training

    Falls are the leading cause of serious injury in older adults. We train your balance reactions, strengthen the legs and hips, and advise on footwear and simple home changes that dramatically reduce risk.

  4. Progress reviews and independence

    We measure improvement at every stage and progressively hand control back to you, with a simple, sustainable home exercise routine to protect your gains for the long term.

Did you know? Research consistently shows that targeted strength and balance training can reduce falls in older adults by around a third. The single biggest predictor of success is not age or diagnosis, it is consistency.

Physiotherapy at Home for Seniors

For many elderly patients, travelling to a clinic is the single biggest barrier to getting care. Our home care physiotherapy service removes that barrier entirely, bringing the same expert treatment to homes across Kakkanad, Kochi and Ernakulam. Home visits carry a real clinical advantage too. They let us assess the exact environment where most falls happen, your own bathroom, staircase and bedroom, and make practical, tailored recommendations on the spot rather than in the abstract.

Home sessions are especially valuable after a hospital stay, a fracture, a stroke or joint replacement surgery, when early, guided movement makes an enormous difference to the final result but travelling is difficult or unsafe.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is physiotherapy safe for very elderly patients?

Yes. Treatment is always tailored to each person's medical condition and fitness level. Gentle, graded exercise is one of the safest and most effective interventions for older adults, and we coordinate with your doctor whenever needed.

How often will my parent need sessions?

Most seniors start with two to three sessions per week, reducing as strength and confidence improve. After the initial assessment we will give you a clear, realistic plan and timeline.

Do you provide home visits for elderly patients in Kochi?

Yes. Home care physiotherapy is one of our core services, covering Kakkanad, Kochi and surrounding areas of Ernakulam. Call +91 80894 14419 to arrange a home assessment.

My father is recovering from a fracture. When should rehab start?

Usually as early as your surgeon or doctor allows. Early, guided movement helps restore strength and confidence and reduces complications. We work alongside your medical team to start at the right time.

Can physiotherapy really reduce the risk of falling?

Yes. Structured strength and balance training is one of the most evidence-backed ways to prevent falls, alongside footwear advice and simple home modifications, all of which we provide.
