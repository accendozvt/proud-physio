Stop Pain Before It Starts

Most desk-related neck and back pain is not caused by a single injury. It builds slowly, from a monitor set too low, a chair without support, hours without movement and poor recovery at night. Each factor seems harmless on its own, but together they load the same joints and muscles day after day until, eventually, something complains. By the time pain appears, the habits behind it are often years deep, which is exactly why catching them early matters so much.

Prevention is not about doing more. It is about doing a few things differently. A monitor raised to eye level, a chair adjusted to support your lower back, a two-minute movement break every hour and a slightly earlier bedtime can, together, change how your body feels within weeks. Our job is to identify precisely which small changes will make the biggest difference for you, and then help you make them stick.

How the Programme Works

Every plan is built around your real routine, not an idealised one. Here is how a typical journey with us unfolds:

  1. Ergonomic assessment

    We review your workstation, whether in an office near Infopark or at home, checking chair height, monitor position, keyboard placement and how you actually sit through the day. You get clear, practical corrections you can apply immediately.

  2. Posture and movement screening

    Dr. Noora assesses your posture, muscle balance and movement patterns to spot the early warning signs of neck, shoulder and back problems before they become painful.

  3. Personalised lifestyle plan

    You receive a realistic plan covering desk stretches, activity targets, sleep hygiene and general nutrition guidance for tissue healing, energy and weight management, built around your actual schedule, not an ideal one.

  4. Follow-up and habit building

    Habits take time. We review your progress, adjust the plan as your routine changes and keep you accountable until healthy movement becomes automatic.

Did you know? Studies show that simple ergonomic adjustments combined with regular movement breaks can cut work-related neck and back pain significantly. Five minutes of guided correction often prevents months of treatment later.

Frequently Asked Questions

I have no pain yet. Is an ergonomic assessment still worth it?

Absolutely. Prevention is the whole point. Correcting your workstation and posture before symptoms appear is far easier, cheaper and quicker than treating established neck or back pain later.

Can you assess my work-from-home setup in Kochi?

Yes. We provide guidance for home workstations across Kakkanad, Kochi and Ernakulam, either at our clinic using photos and measurements of your setup, or through a home visit. Call +91 80894 14419 to arrange one.

Do you provide diet charts or medical nutrition therapy?

We provide general nutrition guidance to support tissue healing, energy and healthy weight as part of your physiotherapy programme. For complex medical dietary needs, we will happily coordinate with your doctor or a clinical dietician.

How long before I notice a difference?

Many people feel less stiffness and fewer end-of-day aches within two to three weeks of correcting their setup and adding regular movement breaks. Lasting change comes as the new habits settle in, which is why we include follow-up support.

Is this useful for students and homemakers, or only IT professionals?

It is useful for anyone whose daily routine loads the same joints and muscles, including students hunched over books, homemakers on their feet all day and working parents juggling both. The advice is tailored to your specific routine.
