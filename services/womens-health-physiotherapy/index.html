Women's Health Physiotherapy

Private, sensitive care from a female physiotherapist, supporting women through pregnancy, postnatal recovery, pelvic floor problems and every stage of life.

Confidential, personalised women's health physiotherapy with a female physiotherapist, at our clinic and in your home.

Many women in Kakkanad and Kochi quietly live with problems they never mention to anyone: leaking urine when they laugh, cough or sneeze, a persistent gap in the tummy months after delivery, back and pelvic pain that flares through pregnancy, or discomfort that lingers long after a C-section. These issues are extremely common, but common is not the same as normal, and they are certainly not something you simply have to accept for the rest of your life.

Women's health physiotherapy at Proud Physio & Wellness is led personally by Dr. Noora Ameen TA (PT), a qualified female physiotherapist. Every consultation is private, unhurried and handled with complete sensitivity, so you can discuss intimate concerns openly, without embarrassment, and receive genuinely personalised care. Because your physiotherapist is a woman, there is no awkwardness in describing what is really happening with your body, and no detail is ever dismissed as just part of being a mother.

Specialist Care for Women, by a Woman

Women's bodies change enormously through pregnancy, birth and menopause, yet these transitions are rarely given the dedicated rehabilitation they deserve. A female physiotherapist understands both the physical and the emotional side of these changes, and that understanding matters. It is far easier to talk honestly about continence, intimacy or a body that no longer feels like your own when the person guiding your recovery is a woman who treats these concerns as everyday clinical matters, not something to be hurried past.

Whether you are expecting your first baby, recovering from delivery, or navigating the joint pain and bone changes of menopause, pelvic floor physiotherapy and targeted, progressive exercise can transform how you feel day to day. The aim is not simply to reduce symptoms but to give you back full confidence in your body, so you can lift your child, exercise, laugh and live without hesitation.

Conditions We Help With

Most women who come to us arrive with more than one concern, and the concerns overlap. Weak pelvic floor muscles, a separated tummy and back pain often travel together after childbirth, so we assess the whole picture rather than treating a single symptom. We regularly help with:

Pelvic floor dysfunction Urinary incontinence Pregnancy back & pelvic girdle pain Prenatal & antenatal fitness Postnatal recovery Diastasis recti (abdominal separation) Post C-section rehabilitation Menopause-related aches & stiffness Core weakness after delivery

If you recognise yourself in even one of these, it is worth a conversation. Many of these conditions respond remarkably well to physiotherapy, and the sooner they are addressed, the quicker and more complete the recovery tends to be.

Leaking, pain and a weakened core after childbirth are common, but they are treatable. You do not have to simply rest and hope they fade.

What Women's Health Physiotherapy Involves

Every programme begins with listening, not exercise. Here is how a typical journey with Dr. Noora unfolds:

  1. Confidential assessment

    A private, one-to-one consultation covering your history, delivery details, symptoms and goals. Nothing is rushed, and nothing is dismissed as normal after childbirth. You set the pace, and every question is welcome.

  2. Personalised treatment plan

    Your programme may combine pelvic floor retraining, safe core reactivation, posture correction, gentle strengthening and hands-on pain relief, all adapted to your stage of pregnancy or recovery and paced to your comfort.

  3. Safe exercise in pregnancy

    We guide you through trimester-appropriate exercise that eases back and pelvic pain, maintains fitness and prepares your body for delivery, with no guesswork about what is safe at each stage.

  4. Postnatal rebuilding

    From around six weeks after delivery, or earlier with your doctor's clearance, we progressively rebuild your core, close diastasis recti, restore continence and get you confidently back to walking, lifting and exercise.

Postpartum recovery is not a luxury. In Kerala, new mothers are often told simply to rest and wait. But research shows that structured postnatal physiotherapy speeds recovery, prevents long-term pelvic floor problems and protects your back for the years of lifting and carrying ahead. Frequently Asked Questions

Is physiotherapy safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Pregnancy physiotherapy is safe and strongly recommended when guided by a qualified physiotherapist. We use trimester-appropriate techniques to ease back pain, pelvic girdle pain and swelling, and coordinate with your gynaecologist whenever needed.

When can I start physiotherapy after a C-section?

Gentle breathing, circulation and posture work can begin within days of delivery. Structured core and scar rehabilitation usually starts around six weeks, once your doctor gives clearance. We tailor every step to your healing.

Will I be treated by a female physiotherapist?

Yes. All women's health consultations at Proud Physio & Wellness are handled personally by Dr. Noora Ameen TA (PT), a qualified female physiotherapist, in a private and comfortable setting. Call +91 80894 14419 to book.

Can physiotherapy help with urinary leaking after childbirth?

Yes. Urinary incontinence after delivery is very common and highly treatable. Structured pelvic floor retraining strengthens the muscles that control your bladder and, for most women, significantly reduces or resolves leaking without surgery.

My delivery was years ago. Is it too late to fix diastasis recti or a weak core?

It is never too late. Many women see real improvement in abdominal separation, core strength and back pain even years after childbirth. A personalised assessment will show exactly what is achievable in your case.
